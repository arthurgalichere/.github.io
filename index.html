Arthur Galichère

Assistant Professor (Teaching Focussed)

University of Warwick · PhD in Economics (Glasgow)

I am an Assistant Professor at the University of Warwick studying macroeconomics. My main research interests focus on themes related to financial crises and macroeconomic policies, such as asset pricing, monetary policy, heterogeneous investments, financial intermediation and stability. I design theoretical models and apply empirical methods to evaluate the effects of asset price bubbles on the economy to provide policy recommendations. My job market paper studies the dynamic ownership of bubbles and its effects on the real economy and financial stability. In contrast, my current projects focus on the Bayesian estimation of DSGE models with stock market bubbles and nominal rigidities.

I joined the University of Warwick in September 2021 as a Teaching Fellow in Macroeconomics near the completion of my PhD at the Adam Smith Business School, University of Glasgow. Following my graduation in 2022, I took on the role of lecturer for the second-year core Macroeconomics module, anchoring student learning in the foundational architectures of advanced macroeconomic research.

In 2024, I was promoted to Assistant Professor (Teaching Focussed), expanding my leadership to the optional core Advanced Macroeconomics module for the MSc in Economics. My pedagogical strategy aims to fully equip students with data analysis systems, theoretical frameworks, and simulation techniques needed to become highly independent analysts or continue directly onto MRes and PhD tracks.
